## 1. What are the requirements to enroll at Pitcairn Flight Academy?

To enroll in our flight training program, you need to meet the following basic requirements:

- Be at least 16 years old to start training and 17 to earn a private pilot certificate.
- Have a valid government-issued photo ID.
- Pass a medical exam conducted by an FAA-authorized aviation medical examiner (AME).

If you’re unsure where to start, our team can help guide you through the process.

## 2. How long does it take to complete flight training?

The length of your training depends on your schedule and how often you fly. On average:

- **Private Pilot License (PPL):** 6–12 months with consistent training.
- **Commercial Pilot License (CPL):** 1–2 years after earning your PPL.

Flying regularly is key to completing your training efficiently. We recommend 2–3 lessons per week for steady progress.

## 3. What training materials or resources are provided?

At Pitcairn Flight Academy, we provide comprehensive training resources, including:

- Access to detailed course materials and handbooks.
- Online tools and apps for studying aviation concepts.
- Simulators for practicing flight maneuvers and procedures.

These resources are designed to complement your hands-on training and help you excel.

## 4. What type of aircraft will I train in?

At Pitcairn Flight Academy, we use modern, well-maintained aircraft for training. Our fleet includes:

- **Cessna 172:** Ideal for beginners with simple controls and an excellent safety record.
- **Piper Archer:** Great for cross-country flying and advanced training.

Our instructors will ensure you’re comfortable and confident in the aircraft you fly.

## 5. Do I need to have any prior experience?

No prior flying experience is required to start your training at Pitcairn Flight Academy. Our program is designed to teach you everything from the basics to advanced flight skills.

## 6. What kind of career opportunities can I pursue after flight school?

Graduating from our flight academy opens doors to various aviation careers, including:

- Commercial airline pilot
- Corporate or charter pilot
- Flight instructor
- Cargo pilot

Our team will help you explore your options and guide you toward achieving your career goals.

Getting started is easy:

1. Contact us to schedule an introductory flight.
2. Meet with one of our instructors to discuss your goals.
3. Complete enrollment and schedule your first lesson.
